# Safety


### Pathways of Exposure
#### Absorbtion
- Direct intake of harmful substances through membraneous tissues -- the skin, eyes, nose and mouth  

#### Ingestion
- Intake of harmful substances by swallowing; while we often think of acute, large-volume ingestion (like when children were eating Tide Pods), even trace amounts can be highly dangerous if a substance is toxic enough  

#### Inhalation
- Exposure to harmful substances through breathing them in, rapidly exposing the nose, mouth, throat, airways and lungs

#### What are the risks?
-  Varies widely by product, but oil-based finishes, especially those containing solvents (thinners) have *numerous* serious risks  
-  Waterborne/water-based and wax-based finishes are generally much safer, but no product is completely safe (even too much water can hurt you!).  
-  Refer to MSDS for each product you use or, at the very least, read the label and follow the instructions (including for PPE) rigorously


#### Creating a Health & Safety Binder
- Every shop should have one -- no matter how many people use it or how big it is; accidents and spills can happen at any time
- Create a main page with phone numbers for poison control, manufacturers' emergency hotlines and your personal and health information
  - Some harmful effects can greatly worsen existing conditions; if overexposure to a product can cause an increased heart rate and you have a chronic heart condition, this is extremely important for emergency services to know
  - Consider what information EMS would need to know that you wouldn't be able to provide if unconscious
**  - In an emergency, leave the binder open to this page in an obvious place, even if you're fully conscious and aware -- that can change quickly in a poisoning emergency**
- Include MSDS for every item already in your shop, and add the MSDS for each new product that enters it

#### Mitigation Techniques
**Eliminate the Hazard**
- Choose a different product  
- Hire a professional   

**Change the Environment**
- Increase ventilation or air circulation  
- Do it outside  
- Look into alternative spaces (rentals, community spaces, etc.)  

**PPE**
- PPE is your last resort, meant to mitigate any remaining risk as much as possible, but it is the most prone to failure and is seldom able to protect you 100%, especially with hobby/DIY-market products

#### Personal Protection Equipment (PPE)
**Absorbtion mitigations:** long sleeves; gloves (nitrile only, since latex and vinyl can allow some solvents to pass through); coveralls; aprons; etc.  
**Ingestion mitigations:** keep all food and drink in sealed containers and do not unseal  them while finishing products are in use; wash hands before touching your mouth or nose; do not use any containers for food or food preparation that you have used for finishing  
**Inhalation mitigations:** wear a properly fitting half- or full-face respirator with appropriate cartridges (generally Organic Vapor cartridges, but for bleaches and acids you'll want to use an Acids cartridge); wear a particulate mask with a minimum rating of N95 or KN95 when sanding or removing material; work outside or in a space with a dedicated fume extraction system if wearing a respirator is not possible or if the effects of VOCs are felt even with a respirator (suggesting the respirator does not fit you well)  

**PPE Care**
- Gloves should be used only once and then disposed of -- do not try to save them because you've only been wearing them for a couple of minutes and now suddenly have to go to the bathroom and want to use the same pair when you get back; putting on a used pair of gloves exposes you to the harm the gloves were helping you avoid.
- Remove gloves safely -- with your dominant hand, pinch the glove near the wrist of your non-dominant hand and pull it up and off of that hand, then ball that glove up in the palm of your dominant hand and with your non-dominant hand pinch your remaining glove near the wrist and pull it off over both the balled-up glove and your hand. Tie the top of your glove 'package' up.
- Once opened, OV cartridges last 6 months or for 40 hours of use (whichever comes first) before needing replacement. However, if stored in a 'sinky' shop (i.e. if your shop smells 'fumey'), they will need replacement sooner.
- Removing your OV cartridges and placing them in a ziplock bag between uses can help prolong their life, but it is best to just replace them every 6 months.
- When replacing your OV cartridges, or approximately every 10 hours of use, you should remove the cartridges from your respirator and wash the rubber body of the respirator with dish soap and warm water, then hang to air dry.
- If sharing a respirator, it should be washed with dish soap and warm water between uses -- avoid the use of isopropyl alcohol or other solvents for cleaning as they can degrade the material of the respirator, depending on what it's made of.
- Aprons and coveralls should be used only for finishing
- **Any PPE with solvents, oils or other flammable materials on them should only be washed by hand, in cool water, and hung to dry; these items should never placed in a washing machine or dryer.**

#### Disposal
- Cloths, rags and applicators should be laid flat outdoors to dry before being disposed of, or after cleaning (unless water-based finish was used and cleaned with soap and water)
- If storage indoors is required, cloths, rags and applicators that have been exposed to flammable solvents and oil-based finishes should be submerged in water in a sealed metal container until disposal at an appropriate facility.
- ***Textiles that have been splashed, spilled on, or soiled by oil-based or flammable finishes should never be placed in household garbage unless they have been allowed to thoroughly dry. It is **always** best to dispose of these items at a dump or transfer station, regardless of whether they're dry or not.***

#### Essential Safety Equipment
- Finishing should only take place in spaces where the following safety equipment is available:
- Fire Extinguisher with an appropriate suppressant for the chemicals you're using (or an ABC extinguisher)
- Comprehensive First Aid Kit
- Eye Wash Bottle or Station
- Appropriate PPE
- Spill Kit (a bin, dustpan and brush, sweeping compound, and garbage bag)
- Outside place to lay applicators to dry, or waterproof metal container that can be sealed
- Way to store extra finish or to store finish if the original container fails
- Adequate ventilation

#### CCWC Shop Rules Around Finishing
- **Only** waterborne or non-solvent-based and non-penetrating oils and waxes may be used in the shop; solvent-based finishes and penetrating oils can be used outside in appropriate weather so long as proper disposal of applicators is ensured (seal in plastic bag with water and no air, then place in garbage OR lay flat to dry completely outdoors)
- **Only** use non-waterborne finishes if you know where the safety equipment is and that it is in good working order
